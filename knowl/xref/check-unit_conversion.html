Check for Understanding 3.2.11.

Neil bought 7 quarts of juice for a party. One glass holds 8 fluid ounces of juice.
🔗
The total number of full glasses that he can fill is .
🔗
Answer.
\(28\)
🔗
🔗
Solution.
Using dimensional analysis and starting with the number of quarts of juice:
🔗
\begin{equation*} 7\;\text{quarts} \cdot \frac{32\;\text{ounces}}{1\;\text{quart}} \cdot \frac{1\;\text{glass}}{8\;\text{ounces}} = 28 \end{equation*}
