Example 2.1.25. Multiply Fractions.

Multiply \(\frac{3}{10} \cdot 3\frac{5}{6}\text{.}\)
Solution.
We first convert the one mixed number factor, \(3\frac{5}{6}\text{,}\) into an improper fraction:
\begin{equation*} 3\frac{5}{6} = \frac{6\cdot 3 + 5}{6} = \frac{18 + 5}{6} = \frac{23}{6}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Now, we “multiply across,” numerators with numerators and denominators with denominators:
\begin{equation*} \frac{3}{10} \cdot 3\frac{5}{6} = \frac{3}{10} \cdot \frac{23}{6} = \frac{3 \cdot 23}{10 \cdot 6} = \frac{69}{60}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We can convert this product to a reduce mixed number:
\begin{equation*} \frac{69}{60} = \frac{60 + 9}{60} = 1\frac{9}{60} = 1\frac{9 \div 3}{60 \div 3} = 1\frac{3}{20}\text{,} \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} \frac{69}{60} = \frac{69 \div 3}{60 \div 3} = \frac{23}{20} = \frac{20 + 3}{20} = 1\frac{3}{20}\text{.} \end{equation*}
