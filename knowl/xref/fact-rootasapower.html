Fact 2.4.4.

For a positive base, \(b\text{,}\) and a positive whole number, \(n\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} b^{\frac{1}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{b}, \end{equation*}
read as “the \(n^\text{th}\) root of \(b\text{.}\)” The \(n^\text{th}\) root of \(b\) is some other number that when raised to the \(n^\text{th}\) power equals \(b\text{.}\)
